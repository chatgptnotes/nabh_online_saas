AAC.1.a - The healthcare services being provided are defined and are in consonance with the needs of the community.

Healthcare Service Definition & Community Needs Assessment

Document No DOC-AAC-001 Version 1.0
Department Quality Department Category SOP
Effective Date 29/12/2025 Review Date 29/12/2026

Purpose

To ensure that the healthcare services provided at Hope Hospital are clearly defined and aligned with the needs of the community it serves, ensuring accessibility, quality, and relevance of services.

Scope

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) applies to all departments and healthcare services provided within Hope Hospital, encompassing inpatient, outpatient, emergency, and specialized care units.

Responsibility

  • Shilpi Singh Baghel (Incharge Nurse): Responsible for implementing this SOP at the ward level, ensuring that patient needs and feedback are communicated effectively to the management.
  • Sharad, Kashish (MRD): Responsible for maintaining accurate records of patient feedback and community needs assessments, ensuring that data is readily available for service planning and improvement.
  • Reception Staff: Responsible for gathering initial patient feedback upon arrival and discharge, providing a direct line of communication with the community.
  • Dr. Rajesh Kumar (Chief Cardiologist): Responsible for aligning cardiology services with community needs, participating in service planning, and reviewing patient outcomes.

Procedure

  1. Service Definition:
    • Each department will clearly define the services it provides, including the target population, service objectives, and expected outcomes.
    • Service definitions will be documented and made accessible to all staff and patients.
  2. Community Needs Assessment:
    • Hope Hospital will conduct regular community needs assessments to identify the healthcare needs and preferences of the population it serves.
    • Assessments will utilize various feedback mechanisms, including patient surveys, community forums, and data from local health agencies.
    • The MRD (Sharad, Kashish) will compile and analyze the assessment data, identifying key trends and unmet needs.
  3. Service Planning:
    • Based on the community needs assessment, Hope Hospital will develop a service plan that outlines how it will address the identified needs.
    • The service plan will be developed in consultation with key stakeholders, including medical staff, nursing staff (Shilpi Singh Baghel), administration, and community representatives.
    • The Cath Lab resources will be considered when planning cardiology services, ensuring optimal utilization of existing infrastructure.
  4. Feedback Mechanisms:
    • Implement multiple feedback mechanisms to continuously monitor patient satisfaction and identify areas for service improvement.
    • Reception staff will gather initial feedback from patients upon arrival and discharge.
    • Patient surveys will be administered regularly to assess satisfaction with specific services.
    • Establish a patient advisory council to provide ongoing feedback and input on service planning.
  5. Service Review:
    • Regularly review the services provided by Hope Hospital to ensure they remain aligned with community needs and best practices.
    • Service reviews will be conducted at least annually and will involve a multidisciplinary team.
    • Review outcomes data, patient feedback, and community needs assessment results to identify areas for improvement.
    • Dr. Rajesh Kumar will participate in cardiology service reviews, ensuring alignment with community health goals.
  6. New Service Development:
    • The organization is not precluded from starting new services based on its judgment, even if not directly identified in community feedback.
    • New services should be evaluated for their potential impact on community health and alignment with the hospital's mission.

Equipment Used

  • Cath Lab (for cardiology services)
  • Ventilators (for critical care)

References

  • SHCO 3rd Edition Interpretation: The healthcare services being provided are defined and are in consonance with the needs of the community.
