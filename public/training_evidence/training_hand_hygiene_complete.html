DOCUMENT 3: TRAINING CONTENT
Training Content

Topic: Hand Hygiene - WHO 5 Moments | Date: 15/08/2025 | Duration: 1.5 Hours

Learning Objectives
  • Understand the importance of hand hygiene in preventing Healthcare Associated Infections (HAIs)
  • Identify the WHO "5 Moments for Hand Hygiene"
  • Demonstrate correct hand washing technique (40-60 seconds)
  • Demonstrate correct hand rubbing technique (20-30 seconds)
  • Understand when to use soap & water vs. alcohol-based hand rub
WHO 5 Moments for Hand Hygiene
Moment 1: Before Touching a Patient
Moment 2: Before Clean/Aseptic Procedures
Moment 3: After Body Fluid Exposure Risk
Moment 4: After Touching a Patient
Moment 5: After Touching Patient Surroundings
Hand Hygiene Techniques
Method Duration When to Use
Hand Washing (Soap & Water) 40-60 seconds Visibly dirty hands, after toilet, before eating
Hand Rubbing (Alcohol-based) 20-30 seconds Routine decontamination, between patient contacts
Key Takeaways
  1. Hand hygiene is the #1 way to prevent HAIs
  2. Remember the WHO 5 Moments
  3. Cover all surfaces including fingertips and thumbs
  4. Keep nails short, no artificial nails or jewelry
  5. Allow hands to dry completely
